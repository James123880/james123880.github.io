Jesus Christ was born around 4 BC in Bethlehem, to the virgin Mary. He grew up in Nazareth and lived a humble and obedient life. At about the age of thirty, He began His public ministry. He preached the kingdom of God, calling people to repent and return to God. Jesus performed many miracles— healing the sick, giving sight to the blind, and even raising the dead. He taught love, forgiveness, and truth, emphasizing the transformation of the heart over outward religion. However, He was opposed by religious leaders. He was betrayed, arrested, and crucified on a cross. There, He gave His life for humanity and prayed, “Father, forgive them.” On the third day, He rose from the dead, appearing to His followers. He later ascended into heaven and promised to return. The life of Jesus reveals the heart of God— redemption, grace, and eternal life.

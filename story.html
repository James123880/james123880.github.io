12 Apostles of Jesus

Peter

Leader of the apostles. A fisherman called by Jesus. Denied Jesus three times but later became a bold preacher. Traditionally believed to have been crucified upside down.

John

Known as the “beloved disciple.” Wrote the Gospel of John, Revelation, and letters. Emphasized love and truth.

James (son of Zebedee)

Brother of John. One of Jesus’ closest disciples. First apostle to be martyred (Acts 12).

Andrew

Peter’s brother. Brought people to Jesus, including Peter. Traditionally crucified on an X-shaped cross.

Philip

Called by Jesus early. Helped bring Nathanael to Christ. Known for asking practical questions.

Bartholomew (Nathanael)

Honest and faithful follower. Traditionally believed to have preached in Asia and been martyred.

Matthew

Former tax collector. Author of the Gospel of Matthew. Left everything to follow Jesus.

Thomas

Known as “Doubting Thomas.” Later showed great faith. Tradition says he preached in India.

James (son of Alphaeus)

Called “James the Less.” Less is known about him, but he faithfully followed Jesus.

Thaddaeus (Jude)

Also called Jude. Asked Jesus important questions. Associated with the Epistle of Jude.

Simon the Zealot

Possibly part of a revolutionary group before following Jesus. Later became a missionary.

Judas Iscariot

Betrayed Jesus for thirty pieces of silver. Later died tragically.
